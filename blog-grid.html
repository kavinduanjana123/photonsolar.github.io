Sri Lanka solar power costs
down 20-pct as rupee strengthens

Sri Lanka's exchange rate drop and reduced manufacturing costs are lowering the expense of solar power systems by around 20% compared to last year, according to Eksath De Silva, an Accounts Manager at E.B. Creasy. The Construction Power and Energy Conference, supported by the company, will take place from July 05 to 07 in Colombo, with expectations of 170 exhibitors and 14,000 visitors. Sri Lanka inks 20-year power
purchase dealwith India's Adani Green

Sri Lanka has signed a 20-year power purchase agreement with India's Adani Green Energy Ltd for two wind power stations in Mannar town and Pooneryn village. Adani Green Energy, part of the Adani Group, will invest $442 million to develop 484 megawatts of wind power capacity. The agreement stipulates a payment of 8.26 cents per kilowatt-hour. Additionally, the Adani Group is investing in a $700 million terminal project at Colombo's largest port. Sri Lanka aims to accelerate renewable energy projects to mitigate risks associated with high imported fuel costs, following power blackouts and fuel shortages during the 2022 economic crisis.

Sri Lanka’s first large-scale solar
project unveiled

Rividhanavi (Private) Limited and the Ceylon Electricity Board (CEB) have inked a 20-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) for a pioneering 100 MW Solar PV Project in Siyambalanduwa, Sri Lanka, marking the nation's first large-scale solar initiative. Led by Lakdhanavi Limited, the consortium, including Windforce PLC and The Blue Circle Pte Limited, aims to harness local expertise and international capabilities. Spanning 220 hectares in the Monaragala district, the Solar Park Facility will utilize advanced photovoltaic technology with battery storage. The project, valued at $150 million, includes infrastructure enhancements and is slated to be operational by mid-2025, contributing significantly to the government's target of 70% renewable power generation by 2030.
